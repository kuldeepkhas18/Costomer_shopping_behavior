#Coatomer_shopping_behavior(Interactive Dashboard creation using MS Power BI)

## Project Objective
This project focuses on analyzing customer shopping behavior in the retail/e-commerce domain using data-driven techniques. The goal is to uncover meaningful insights about customer purchasing patterns, spending habits, and interaction with marketing strategies.

An interactive dashboard is built to visualize key insights such as customer trends, product performance, payment behavior, and seasonal demand. This helps businesses make informed decisions to improve customer retention, optimize marketing strategies, and increase revenue.


## Business Questions Solved
1.Which product category generates the highest revenue?

2.Do discounts actually increase purchase value and total revenue?

3.What is the total revenue generated by male and female customers?

4.Which customers used discounts but still spent more than the average purchase amount?

5.Which are the top 5 and bottom 5 products based on average review ratings?

6.How does shipping type (Standard vs Express) impact average purchase value and total revenue?

7.Do subscribed customers spend more compared to non-subscribed customers?

8.Which products have the highest dependency on discounts (highest discount usage %)?

9.How can customers be segmented into New, Returning, and Loyal based on purchase history?

10.What are the top 3 most purchased products within each category?

11.Are repeat buyers (more than 5 previous purchases) more likely to subscribe?

12.Which age group contributes the most to total revenue?



## Project Insights
-A few product categories contribute the majority of total revenue

-Top-performing products drive most of the sales

-Repeat and loyal customers generate higher revenue than new customers

-Discounts are not always effective and can reduce profitability

-Some products are highly dependent on discounts for sales

-Customer segmentation helps in better targeting and personalization

-Subscribed customers tend to spend more than non-subscribed customers

-Shipping type influences purchase value (faster shipping → higher spending)

-Customer preferences vary across different payment methods

-Sales show clear seasonal trends and demand patterns

-Product ratings impact customer purchase decisions

-Different age groups contribute differently to total revenue



## Final Conclusion

This project demonstrates how customer shopping behavior analysis can transform raw data into actionable business insights. By identifying high-revenue categories, valuable customer segments, and key purchasing patterns, the analysis highlights where businesses should focus their efforts. It reveals that loyal and subscribed customers contribute significantly to revenue, while discounts need to be applied strategically to avoid profit loss. Additionally, factors such as product performance, shipping preferences, seasonal trends, and customer demographics play a crucial role in influencing sales. Overall, the project emphasizes the importance of data-driven decision-making to optimize marketing strategies, improve customer retention, and maximize business growth.
